Frequently Asked Questions

Please contact the executive director's email: pollard@oakland.edu

Historically at Oakland, the strength of the union has provided all OU faculty with professionally competitive salaries and benefit packages. Further, any faculty member who feels s/he has been treated unfairly by the administration has recourse to AAUP support. The AAUP can intercede and/or mediate in any number of ways, from attempting to generate informal dialogue right on up to the filing of a formal grievance. The AAUP, most often through the Executive Director, can advise faculty members on many issues, offering a perspective which may not be available within the faculty member's immediate department. Also, the AAUP continually strives to insure a professional environment which promotes and protects the academic freedom of all faculty. Members of the AAUP participate in the selection of AAUP officers and negotiating teams, and can participate in the decisions that guide the direction of the union.

If you have a concern that may be an issue covered by the Faculty Agreement, you can contact the AAUP Office. You can call the office at (248) 370-2005; fax a question to (248) 370-2004; or email a question to the Executive Director. If you prefer to visit the AAUP office, it is located on the second floor of Pryale Hall Room 201. Pryale Hall is located at the eastern end of the large parking lot behind O'Dowd Hall. It is across the street from the Heating Plant and the Recreation Center parking lot.

The most obvious problems which the AAUP can help with involve contract violations. Faculty members should be somewhat familiar with the contract and the rights and responsibilities defined in it. The officers of the AAUP, most often the Executive Director, are readily accessible to explain the language in more detail. If you are denied a leave, phased retirement, sabbatical, or any formal request, or if a formal request for any of the above is not responded to in timely fashion, the AAUP can initiate a discussion, determine whether a contract violation has occurred, and if a contract violation has occurred, file a grievance. More broadly, though, the AAUP may be able to help you whenever you feel you have been mistreated by the administration.

In any complex institution personnel problems will occur from time to time. In universities these might include conflicts between faculty and students, faculty and faculty, and faculty and administrators. The formal grievance process delineated in Article XXIV of the 2003-2006 Faculty Agreement exists to resolve conflicts between faculty and administrators. The University, College, schools, and departments have grievance procedures to address the other two types of personnel conflicts involving faculty with students or other faculty. For every type of problem an effort should be made by all parties to solve the problem at the lowest possible level which requires effective communication including careful attentive listening. The contractual grievance process, as well as others, is designed to protect the less authoritative party against arbitrary decisions by the more authoritative party that could jeopardize the former's rights to fair treatment. The Article XXIV grievance procedure is limited to resolution of disagreements and problems faculty “may have in relation to employment by Oakland, arising from the application, or interpretation of this Agreement.” The Faculty Agreement defines who can teach credit courses; perform professional library service; the process and criteria for awarding academic titles, reviewing, reappointing, and promoting faculty; methods and levels of compensation of faculty; conditions and processes for layoff and recall of faculty; discipline and discharge procedures; fringe benefit entitlements of faculty; conditions and policies for paid and unpaid leaves; rights and responsibilities of faculty and chairpersons; and past practice. Past practices include the principles of academic freedom, and existing procedures, policies, and practices as outlined by the constitutions of the University Senate, schools, and college. Discrimination by reason of age, race, creed, marital status, color, sex, religion, national origin, citizenship, sexual orientation, political affiliation, or handicap is prohibited. If any faculty member believes the administration has violated any provision of the faculty agreement including but not limited to those outlined above, he/she should contact the Grievance Officer or AAUP Chapter Executive Director. These officials will discuss the potential contract violations and explore informal means for resolution of the problem. Should such informal means fail, the Grievance Officer will file a formal grievance on your behalf. All faculty have the right to file a grievance whether or not the AAUP officials agree there has been a contract violation. Administrative reprisals against anyone filing a grievance are violations not only of our contract but also of state and federal law. Since department chairs are members of the bargaining unit, faculty may not file formal grievances against department chairs. It may still be possible to grieve the dean for failure to properly supervise a chair. TIME LIMITS. Grievances must be filed in a timely manner. Grievances must be filed within 30 calendar days of the incident to be grieved or within 30 calendar days after the faculty member of the Association should have known thereof and no later than 90 calendar days of the incident. Once the Association has been notified of a potential grievance, it may be able to obtain extensions to the formal filing deadline to explore informal resolution methods. STEPS IN THE GRIEVANCE PROGRESS. The formal grievance process has three steps. A step one hearing is usually held by a staff member of the Office of Academic Affairs. Whether the Grievance Officer filed the grievance on your behalf or you filed it, you are entitled to have and strongly urged to have a representative of the Association present at the Step One hearing. Within 10 days of the hearing the administration will provide a written response. If the grievant is not satisfied with the Step One response, he/she may ask the Association to request a Step Two hearing. This request must be made within 10 days of receipt of the Step One Response. A Step Two hearing is usually conducted by representatives of the Office of Academic Affairs and one academic dean. The Association Grievance Officer will present the case for the grievant although the grievant is usually present. The administration will provide a written response within 10 days of the hearing. If the Association is not satisfied with the Step Two response, it may request binding arbitration. For tenure arbitration the arbitrator will be selected from the panel of arbitrators listed in paragraph 183 of the Faculty Agreement. For other grievances the arbitrator will be chosen by mutual agreement between the Association and the administration or the procedures of the America Arbitration Association or the Federal Mediation Conciliation Service. Arbitrations are scheduled by mutual agreement between the Association, administration, and arbitrator and last from a few hours to a few days. The arbitrator has 30 days to render a final decision.

A good place to start is your department chair and/or your academic unit's AAUP representative. If you have additional questions, contact the AAUP Chapter Executive Director . chances are that your question has been asked many times before and an answer will be quickly found. On the chance that your question raises new concerns or possibilities, contacting the AAUP Executive Committee can insure that it will be included in the discussions for the next round of negotiations.
